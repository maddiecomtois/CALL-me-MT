Background

You are invited to participate in this study on machine translation and language learning, which aims to test if quick and easy access to translations enhances the readerâ€™s experience. When reading in a foreign language, learners usually have to stop in the middle of a sentence to look up a word or phrase that they do not know. This lack of vocabulary and start-stop process disrupts the ability to read fluently in a foreign language, which can be frustrating to the reader. Computer-Assisted Language Learning (CALL) can help foreign language learners in providing technology to enhance their learning experience.

Instructions

You are asked to spend at least five minutes reading public domain stories in a foreign language of your choice, looking up words or phrases that are unfamiliar to you. After spending as much time as you like reading and translating the text, we ask you to fill out an anonymous survey about your experience. This survey should take no more than 15 minutes and is entirely anonymous. When you come across a word or phrase that you do not know during your reading, highlight the word or phrase with your mouse to display the translation.

This platform provides text in the following languages:

  1. Chinese (for English speakers)
  2. Czech (for English speakers)
  3. English (for French speakers)
  4. French (for English speakers)
  5. German (for English speakers)
  6. Italian (for English speakers)
  7. Japanese (for English speakers)
  8. Portuguese (for English speakers)
  9. Spanish (for English speakers)

How to use the platform:

  1. Select the language you are learning from the menu on the left side of the screen
  2. Select a story from the available options provided in the submenu (longer stories take longer to load)
  3. Read as much of the story as you wish to improve your foreign langauge reading skills
  4. If you come across a word or phrase you do not know, highlight the text with your mouse. A translation will be displayed above the text when the mouse is released
  5. Happy reading!
